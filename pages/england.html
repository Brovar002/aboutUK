Geography

England occupies the southern and central portions of the island of Great Britain. It shares land borders with Scotland to the north and Wales to the west. The English Channel separates it from France to the south — they are connected by the Channel Tunnel, opened in 1994.

The landscape is varied: flat plains and fens in the east, rolling hills of the Cotswolds in the centre, rugged moorlands in the south-west (Dartmoor, Exmoor), and the dramatic Lake District mountains in the north-west.

Major rivers include the Thames (flowing through London), the Severn (the longest in the UK), and the Trent. The highest point is Scafell Pike (978m) in the Lake District.

History

1066
Norman Conquest — William the Conqueror defeats King Harold at the Battle of Hastings, transforming English language and society.
1215
Magna Carta — King John signs the Great Charter at Runnymede, establishing that even kings are subject to the law.
1760s
Industrial Revolution — England leads the world's transformation from farming to factory production, powered by steam and coal.
1940–45
WWII — England withstands the Blitz under Winston Churchill. The Battle of Britain in 1940 was a turning point.

Culture & Traditions

🍵
Afternoon Tea

Tea, sandwiches, and scones served at 3–5pm. A ritual since the 1840s.

🎆
Guy Fawkes Night

Bonfires and fireworks on November 5th, commemorating the Gunpowder Plot.

🏏
Cricket

England's national sport. International matches are called "Test Matches" and can last 5 days!

🎾
Wimbledon

The world's oldest tennis tournament, held in London every July since 1877.

Famous People

✍️
William Shakespeare (1564–1616) — Playwright and poet from Stratford-upon-Avon. Wrote Hamlet, Romeo and Juliet, Macbeth, and 34 other plays.
🍎
Isaac Newton (1643–1727) — Mathematician from Lincolnshire who formulated the laws of gravity and motion.
🎵
The Beatles (1960s) — John Lennon, Paul McCartney, George Harrison, Ringo Starr — from Liverpool. Changed music forever.
📖
Charles Dickens (1812–1870) — Author from Portsmouth who wrote Oliver Twist, A Christmas Carol, and Great Expectations.

💡 Interesting Facts about England

  • → England has the world's oldest parliamentary democracy.
  • → The English language has over 600,000 words — more than any other language.
  • → Football (soccer) was invented in England. The Football Association was founded in 1863.
  • → Stonehenge, built around 3000 BC, predates the Egyptian pyramids.
  • → Oxford University (founded ~1096) is the oldest English-speaking university in the world.
