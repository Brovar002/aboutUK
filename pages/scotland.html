Geography

Scotland occupies the northern third of the island of Great Britain. It has three main regions: the Highlands (wild mountains and lochs in the north), the Central Belt (where most people live, including Edinburgh and Glasgow), and the Southern Uplands (rolling moorland).

Scotland has over 790 islands, including the Hebrides on the west coast and Shetland and Orkney in the far north. Ben Nevis (1,345m) is the highest mountain in the entire UK. Loch Lomond is the largest loch by surface area.

History

1314
Battle of Bannockburn — Robert the Bruce defeats the English army, securing Scottish independence.
1707
Act of Union — Scotland and England unite to form the Kingdom of Great Britain.
1745
Jacobite Rising — Bonnie Prince Charlie's failed attempt to reclaim the British throne.
1999
Devolution — Scottish Parliament re-established in Edinburgh after nearly 300 years.

Culture & Traditions

🎆
Hogmanay

Scottish New Year (Dec 31). More important than Christmas. First-footing and "Auld Lang Syne".

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿
Kilts & Tartan

Each Scottish clan has its own tartan (plaid pattern). Kilts are worn at formal occasions and Highland Games.

🍽️
Haggis

Scotland's national dish: sheep offal with oatmeal, served with "neeps" (turnip) and "tatties" (potato).

🥃
Scotch Whisky

Scotland produces over 40 million casks of whisky, exported to 175 countries. "Single malt" whisky is a Scottish invention.

Famous Scots

💉
Alexander Fleming (1881–1955) — Bacteriologist from Ayrshire who discovered penicillin in 1928, saving hundreds of millions of lives.
✍️
Robert Burns (1759–1796) — Scotland's national poet. Wrote "Auld Lang Syne" and "A Red, Red Rose". Burns Night is celebrated on January 25th.
🔬
James Watt (1736–1819) — Engineer from Greenock who improved the steam engine, powering the Industrial Revolution.
