Geography

Northern Ireland is the only part of the UK that shares a land border with another country — the Republic of Ireland to the south and west. It is situated in the north-east of the island of Ireland.

The landscape includes Lough Neagh — the largest freshwater lake in the UK — the stunning Antrim Coast, the Mourne Mountains, and of course, the famous Giant's Causeway.

The Giant's Causeway

Northern Ireland's most famous landmark is the Giant's Causeway — approximately 40,000 interlocking basalt columns on the Antrim coast. The columns were formed about 60 million years ago when lava from a volcanic eruption cooled rapidly.

Irish legend says the causeway was built by the giant Finn McCool so he could walk to Scotland to fight a Scottish giant. The Giant's Causeway was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1986.

The Titanic

The RMS Titanic, the world's most famous ship, was built in Belfast at the Harland & Wolff shipyard. The ship was launched in 1911 and sank on its maiden voyage on April 15, 1912, after hitting an iceberg. Over 1,500 people died.

Today, Titanic Belfast is the world's largest Titanic visitor experience, built on the exact site where the ship was constructed. It opened in 2012 on the 100th anniversary of the sinking.

Culture & Traditions

☘️
St. Patrick's Day

Celebrated on March 17th. St. Patrick is the patron saint of Ireland. Celebrated worldwide with parades and green clothing.

🌳
Dark Hedges

A stunning beech tree avenue in County Antrim — famously used as the "King's Road" in Game of Thrones.

🎭
Game of Thrones

Northern Ireland was the main filming location for Game of Thrones. Over 26 filming locations can be visited.

🏟️
GAA Sports

Gaelic football and hurling are popular traditional Irish sports played throughout Northern Ireland.
