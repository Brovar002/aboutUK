The Welsh Language (Cymraeg)

Welsh (Cymraeg) is one of Europe's oldest living languages, belonging to the Celtic language family. About 29% of people in Wales speak Welsh. All road signs in Wales are bilingual — in both Welsh and English.

The Welsh word for Wales is Cymru (pronounced "Cum-ree"), meaning "land of compatriots". The language survived centuries of pressure and is now taught in all Welsh schools. There is even a Welsh-language TV channel, S4C.

Some Welsh words:
  • 🐉 Draig = Dragon
  • 🏰 Castell = Castle
  • 🌊 Môr = Sea
  • ⛰️ Mynydd = Mountain
  • 🏡 Cymru = Wales
  • 👋 Bore da = Good morning

Culture & Music

Wales is famous for its choral singing tradition — Welsh male voice choirs are renowned worldwide. The Eisteddfod is a celebrated national festival of poetry, literature, and music dating back to the 12th century.

🎼
Eisteddfod

National festival of Welsh culture, music and poetry. Both a traditional and modern celebration.

🏉
Rugby Union

Wales's national sport. The Welsh rugby team plays in red at the Principality Stadium in Cardiff.

🌼
St. David's Day

March 1st — national day. Welsh people wear daffodils or leeks as national symbols.

🏰
Castle Country

Wales has over 600 castles — more per square mile than any other country in the world.

💡 Interesting Facts about Wales

  • → Wales has more castles per square mile than any other country — over 600!
  • → The world's first passenger railway ran in South Wales in 1807.
  • → Tom Jones, Anthony Hopkins, and Catherine Zeta-Jones are all Welsh.
  • → In 1916, David Lloyd George became the first Welsh Prime Minister of the UK.
  • → Snowdon (Yr Wyddfa) — at 1,085m — is the highest peak in Wales and England.
