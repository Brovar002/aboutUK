The United Kingdom — An Introduction
45 мин 5–6 класс Geography

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Students can name and locate the four countries of the UK on a map.
  • Students know the capital cities of all four nations.
  • Students can describe the UK flag (Union Jack) and what it represents.

📖 Key Vocabulary

  • United Kingdom — страна, состоящая из четырёх наций
  • Capital city — главный город страны
  • Flag / Union Jack — флаг
  • Nation — нация, страна
  • Island — остров

📋 Lesson Plan

Warm-up (5 min)

Show the Union Jack flag. Ask: "What country does this flag belong to?" Elicit student knowledge.

Presentation (15 min)

Use the interactive map to show the four nations. Teach: "England is in the south. Scotland is in the north. Wales is in the west. Northern Ireland is on a separate island." Drill the names and capitals chorally.

Practice (15 min)

Students draw and label a simple UK map. Write the country names, capitals, and one national symbol for each (lion, unicorn, dragon, shamrock).

Production (10 min)

Pair activity: Student A asks "What is the capital of Scotland?" Student B answers "Edinburgh." Rotate through all four nations.

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1: Match the capitals
  1. England → ___________
  2. Scotland → ___________
  3. Wales → ___________
  4. Northern Ireland → ___________
1. London   2. Edinburgh   3. Cardiff   4. Belfast
Exercise 2: True or False?
  1. The UK has five countries. ( )
  2. London is the capital of Scotland. ( )
  3. Wales has a dragon on its flag. ( )
  4. Northern Ireland shares an island with Ireland. ( )
1. False (four)   2. False (London = capital of England)   3. True   4. True
02
British Culture — Tea, Traditions & Symbols
45 мин 5–6 класс Culture

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Students can name at least 5 British cultural traditions.
  • Students understand the difference between British and American English vocabulary.
  • Students can describe a British tradition in 2–3 sentences.

📖 Key Vocabulary

  • Afternoon tea — послеполуденный чай с угощениями
  • Bonfire Night — 5 ноября, ночь фейерверков
  • Double-decker bus — двухэтажный автобус
  • Pub — паб (public house)
  • Queuing — стояние в очереди

✏️ Exercises

Exercise 1: Complete the sentences
  1. British people drink a lot of ___________ (tea / coffee).
  2. London's famous buses are ___________-coloured.
  3. ___________ Night is celebrated on November 5th.
  4. A "pub" is short for "public ___________".
1. tea   2. red   3. Bonfire/Guy Fawkes   4. house

02
UK Geography — Rivers, Mountains & Landscapes
45 мин 7–8 класс Geography

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Students can name the main rivers, mountains, and geographic features of the UK.
  • Students can describe a geographic feature using appropriate vocabulary.
  • Students understand the difference between a loch, lake, river, and mountain.

📖 Key Vocabulary

  • River Thames — flows through London to the North Sea (346 km)
  • River Severn — the UK's longest river (354 km)
  • Ben Nevis — highest mountain in the UK (1,345m, Scotland)
  • Scafell Pike — highest mountain in England (978m)
  • Snowdon / Yr Wyddfa — highest mountain in Wales (1,085m)
  • Loch — Scottish word for lake
  • Highland — высокогорье
  • Peninsula — полуостров (e.g. Cornwall)

✏️ Exercises

Exercise: Complete with the correct word
  1. The ___________ is the longest river in England. (Thames / Severn)
  2. ___________ is the highest mountain in the UK at 1,345 metres.
  3. In Scotland, a lake is called a ___________.
  4. Cornwall is a ___________ in the south-west of England.
  5. The English Channel separates England from ___________.
1. Severn (longest UK) or Thames (longest in England)   2. Ben Nevis   3. loch   4. peninsula   5. France

British History — Key Events & People
45 мин 9 класс History

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Students can place key events in British history on a timeline.
  • Students can describe 3 significant events in British history in English.
  • Students understand the significance of the Magna Carta for democracy.

📜 Key Historical Events Timeline

1066
Norman Conquest — William the Conqueror invades England. Old English + French → Modern English begins.
1215
Magna Carta — The first legal document limiting royal power. Foundation of constitutional democracy.
1564
Shakespeare born — William Shakespeare, the world's greatest playwright, born in Stratford-upon-Avon.
1707
Act of Union — England and Scotland unite to form the Kingdom of Great Britain.
1760s
Industrial Revolution — Britain leads the world into the age of machines, steam, and industry.
1939–45
World War II — Britain withstands German bombing (the Blitz) under PM Winston Churchill.
1948
NHS Founded — National Health Service created. Free healthcare for all UK citizens.

✏️ Discussion Questions

  1. Why was the Magna Carta (1215) important? How did it change the relationship between kings and their people?
  2. What was the Industrial Revolution, and why did it begin in Britain?
  3. What was "the Blitz"? How did the British people respond to the German bombing?
  4. What is the NHS, and why is it considered important to British identity?

British Identity — What does it mean to be British?
45 мин 10–11 класс Culture & Identity

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Students can discuss the concept of national identity in English.
  • Students can compare British and Russian cultural values.
  • Students can write a short essay (150–200 words) about British cultural identity.

📖 Reading Text — British Identity

What makes someone British? This is a question that has no simple answer. Britain is a country of contrasts and contradictions. On one hand, there are ancient traditions: the monarchy, parliamentary democracy, afternoon tea, and cricket. On the other hand, Britain is one of the most diverse and multicultural countries in the world.

British people are often described as reserved and polite — they queue patiently, apologise constantly, and talk about the weather. Yet they also produced punk rock, the Sex Pistols, and some of the world's most provocative artists and writers.

Perhaps what truly defines British identity is a set of shared values: a belief in fair play, freedom of speech, tolerance, and a sense of humour — particularly the ability to laugh at oneself. Whether through Shakespeare, the Beatles, Harry Potter, or the NHS, Britain has given the world ideas, art, and institutions that continue to shape global culture.

✏️ Reading Comprehension

Answer the questions in English:
  1. According to the text, what are two traditional aspects of British culture?
  2. How are British people described in terms of social behaviour?
  3. What values does the text say are important to British identity?
  4. What examples of British cultural contributions to the world are given?
  5. Do you think Russia has similar "defining values"? What are they?

✍️ Writing Task

Write 150–200 words responding to the question: "What are the most interesting or surprising aspects of British culture?"

Use vocabulary from this lesson. Include: at least one comparison with Russian culture, one specific example of a British tradition or cultural product, and your personal opinion.

02
The British Political System — Parliament & Democracy
45 мин 10–11 класс Politics & Society

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Students can explain the structure of the UK political system.
  • Students can describe the roles of the Monarch, Parliament, and Prime Minister.
  • Students can compare the UK and Russian systems of government.

📖 Key Vocabulary

  • Constitutional monarchy — конституционная монархия (монарх — символ, реальная власть у парламента)
  • Parliament — парламент (House of Commons + House of Lords)
  • Prime Minister — премьер-министр (глава правительства)
  • General Election — всеобщие выборы (проводятся каждые 5 лет)
  • Devolution — передача части полномочий Шотландии, Уэльсу, Северной Ирландии

✏️ Discussion

  1. What is the difference between a constitutional monarchy and an absolute monarchy?
  2. Why is Britain called "the Mother of Parliaments"?
  3. What does "devolution" mean? Why might Scotland and Wales want their own parliaments?
  4. Compare the UK system (Prime Minister) and the Russian system (President). What are the key differences?
